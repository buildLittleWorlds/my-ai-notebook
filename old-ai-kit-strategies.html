Reading Strategies Toolkit (Stances & Playbooks)

How Strategies Differ from Methods

  • Strategies = stances and goals (why/when). They braid multiple methods to answer a question.
  • Methods = atomic procedures (how). They generate evidence you’ll use here.

Start with a goal below, then pull the recommended methods from Methods.

Choose Your Aim

  • Debunk / Attribute? Use Forensic (Archaeological).
  • Situate-in-Tradition? Use Gadamer (Dialogical).
  • Expose Contradictions? Use Derrida (Deconstructive).
  • Surface Values & Power? Use Nietzsche (Genealogical).
  • Project Worlds & Ethics? Use Ricoeur (World-Projection).
  • Make It Work? Use Pragmatist (Functional).

Strategy Cards

Gadamer (Dialogical Reading)

Aim: Bring the text into dialogue; surface traditions within the model’s training distribution and attempt a horizon-fusion.

Best when: you want to understand how the text participates in or renews a tradition.

Uses methods: D (Topic Map), E (System Circle), I (World Sketch).

Failure modes: romanticizing “tradition” in the training data; ignoring power asymmetries.

Try it on: Case Studies · Background: Genealogy

Derrida (Deconstructive Reading)

Aim: Track binaries and slippages; stress-test meaning under iterability.

Best when: contradictions or undecidables are suspected.

Uses methods: C (Logical Pass), F (Oppositions), G (Iterability).

Failure modes: over-reading noise; declaring undecidability too early.

Primer: IEP on Derrida · See also: Death of the Author

Nietzsche (Genealogical Reading)

Aim: Map value-claims, power-effects, beneficiaries and exclusions.

Best when: a “neutral” tone masks normative agendas.

Uses methods: H (Value-Claim Map).

Failure modes: flattening nuance; cynicism that forecloses dialogue.

Bridge: When the Machine Speaks

Ricoeur (World-Projection Reading)

Aim: Describe the world the text opens and what kinds of action/self-understanding it enables.

Best when: provenance matters less than appropriation and use.

Uses methods: D (Topic Map), E (System Circle), I (World Sketch).

Failure modes: projecting an implausible or ethically thin world; ignoring constraints.

Background: Distanciation

Forensic (Archaeological Reading)

Aim: Attribute, date, source, and assess AI-likelihood by weighing multi-signal evidence.

Best when: authorship, provenance, or reliability are in question.

Uses methods: A (Transitions), B (Register), C (Logic), E (System), G (Iterability), J (Task Log).

Failure modes: mistaking probability for proof; cherry-picking signals.

See: AI Text Forensics

Pragmatist (Functional Reading)

Aim: Judge the text by what it lets you do—clarity, sufficiency, error-cost, user outcomes.

Best when: working with instructions, briefs, or decision support.

Uses methods: A (Transitions), C (Logic), J (Task Log).

Failure modes: optimizing utility while missing ideology or hidden values.

Try with: the technical-doc examples in Case Studies

Combining Strategies

  • API docs: Forensic → Pragmatist (A, C, E, J).
  • Opinion essay: Nietzsche → Gadamer (H, D, E).
  • Poetry: Derrida → Ricoeur (F, G, I).
