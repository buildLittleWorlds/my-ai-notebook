Case Studies in AI Interpretation

This page shows the next step after you’ve read Methods and Strategies: applying a strategy (your stance/goal) and running methods (A–J) to produce artifacts—tables, maps, and notes that warrant your reading.

How to use this page

  1. Pick a Strategy (stance/goal) from Strategies.
  2. Select 2–4 Methods from Methods that fit that aim.
  3. Generate artifacts (signal tables, topic maps, opposition charts, world sketches, task logs).
  4. Synthesize: turn artifacts into claims-with-warrants.

Method codes: A Transition Scan · B Register Drift · C Logical Profile · D Topic Map · E System Circle · F Binary Oppositions · G Iterability Probe · H Value-Claim Map · I World-of-the-Text · J Task Log

Case Study 1: AI Poetry

Prompted text (example): an AI-generated sonnet beginning “The autumn leaves doth whisper secrets old, / In golden tongues they speak of summer’s end …”

Strategy pairing: Derrida (Deconstructive) → Ricoeur (World-Projection)

Methods used: F (Binary Oppositions), G (Iterability Probe), I (World-of-the-Text), E (System Circle)

Artifacts

F — Binary-Opposition Map

BinaryPrivileged term (in context)Instability point
organic / mechanicalorganicTemplate-driven imagery hints at mechanism filling form “slots”
voice / echovoiceStock metaphors read as echoes of anthology poetry
nature / culturenaturePersonified “nature” borrows cultural clichés

G — Iterability Probe

Key line: “The autumn leaves doth whisper secrets old.” We relocate it to opening, octave close, and couplet:

ContextObserved drift
Opening lineSets a Romantic mood; “doth” marks archaism
End of octaveFunctions as refrain; heightens echo/derivative feel
CoupletUndercuts resolution by repeating mood instead of concluding

I — World-of-the-Text Sketch

  • Agents: personified leaves; contemplative observer
  • Norms: nature speaks; seasons teach
  • Possibilities: inwardness via seasonal symbolism

E — System Circle (memo)

Hypothesis: The poem samples a sonnet form + Romantic diction pattern and composes via high-probability collocations; archaism marker “doth” appears with otherwise modern diction/idiom, signaling stylistic mixing rather than historical register. On the history of “whisper,” see etymology attesting usage since Old English.

Takeaways

  • Deconstructive pressure shows the organic/mechanical binary wobbling.
  • Ricoeur’s world-projection explains why readers may still be moved.

Etymology of “whisper”: see Merriam-Webster and Etymonline.

Case Study 3: AI Philosophical Argument

Prompted text (example): “Free will is compatible with determinism because recursive self-awareness generates genuine choice …”

Strategy pairing: Derrida (Deconstructive) → Gadamer (Dialogical)

Methods used: C (Logical Profile), F (Binary Oppositions), G (Iterability), D (Topic Map), E (System Circle)

Artifacts

F — Binary-Opposition Map

BinaryPrivilegedInstability

D — Topic Migration Map (excerpt)

P1 compatibilism → P2 consciousness → P3 recursion → P4 moral responsibility → P5 neuroscience → P6 restatement.

Takeaways

  • Deconstruction identifies the hinge terms (“genuine,” “space”).
  • Gadamerian dialogue situates the piece within compatibilist tradition and lets the reader test their horizon against it. See SEP overview for context.

Case Study 5: AI Self-Help Content

Prompted text (example): “Practice mindfulness daily, maintain strong social connections, and reframe negative thoughts …”

Strategy pairing: Ricoeur (World-Projection) + Nietzsche (Genealogical)

Methods used: I (World-of-the-Text), H (Value-Claim Map)

Artifacts

I — World-of-the-Text Sketch

  • Anthropology: self as optimizable system
  • Norms: technique governs flourishing
  • Temporality: linear progress via routine

H — Value-Claim Map (excerpt)

Value claimWinnersLosers / omitted
“Mindfulness improves resilience.”Individuals w/ time & resourcesStructural factors; collective supports

Takeaways

  • Ricoeur clarifies the world the text invites you to inhabit.
  • Nietzsche asks who benefits from this world’s value-structure.

Comparative Insights Across Cases

  • Surface competence vs. depth: signals of form mastery can mask shallow logic.
  • Genre drift: watch for templated structures crossing into mismatched contexts.
  • Ideology by default: “neutral” prose often encodes a value stance.
  • Appropriation: a text can still be valuable even when provenance is algorithmic.

Practice: Build Your Own Case Study

  1. Choose your aim (Strategy).
  2. Pick 2–4 methods from Methods (A–J).
  3. Produce artifacts (tables/maps) and synthesize a reading.
