52 A structuralist exegesis of you in Family of the Year’s “Hero”

Not a list but a score. The analysis proceeds in five movements with interludes and counterfigures. Each movement reorganizes image and lyric into a different register of address, testing how the pronoun you is produced and rotated by spectacle, intimacy, economy, memory, and tradition.

Overture: statement of problem and constraints

The pronoun you in “Hero” appears at first as a singular addressee; the video refuses that simplicity. Rodeo, crowd, flag, fence, paycheck, partner, child—each scene installs a second-person function with a different demand. The pronoun is not a person but a seat of demand that slides across these functions as the scene changes. Refusal targets the seat, not the specific occupant.
