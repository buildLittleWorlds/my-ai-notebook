Case Studies in AI Interpretation

Theory becomes useful when applied to real examples. These case studies demonstrate how to apply hermeneutical methods to actual AI texts, showing how different approaches reveal different aspects of meaning.

Case Study 1: AI Poetry

Text: An AI-generated sonnet beginning with "The autumn leaves doth whisper secrets old, / In golden tongues they speak of summer's end..."

Applying Multiple Methods

Forensic Analysis

  • Anachronisms: "Doth" (archaic) with "whisper" (modern colloquial)
  • Source Traces: Combines Shakespearean sonnet structure with Romantic nature imagery
  • AI Signatures: Generic "poetic" language, abstract metaphors without specific grounding
  • Training Data: Likely trained on anthology poetry rather than complete poetic works

Organic/Mechanical Form Analysis

  • Mechanical Aspects: Follows sonnet template rigidly, fills in "slots" with poetic-sounding phrases
  • Missing Organic Unity: No governing idea develops through the poem; each line exists independently
  • Statistical Beauty: Pleasing surface effects without deeper necessity

Ricoeur's World-Projection

  • Projected World: A realm where nature is inherently meaningful and speaks to humans
  • Temporal Structure: Cyclical time (seasons) rather than linear narrative
  • Relationship to Tradition: Assumes romantic/transcendentalist connection to nature
  • Appropriation Possibilities: Can evoke genuine aesthetic response despite algorithmic origin

Interpretive Insights

This case reveals how AI poetry succeeds at surface aesthetics while lacking the deeper intentional unity that defines organic form. Yet its "world" can still move readers, suggesting meaning emerges from reception as much as creation.

Case Study 2: AI News Article

Text: AI-generated article: "Study Shows AI May Transform Healthcare Delivery by 2030, Experts Cautiously Optimistic"

Genealogical Reading (Nietzsche Method)

Embedded Values

  • Techno-Solutionism: Assumes technology is the answer to healthcare problems
  • Progress Narrative: Linear improvement through innovation
  • Expert Authority: Defers to credentialed voices over patient experience
  • Quantification Bias: Privileges measureable outcomes

Power Dynamics

  • Who Benefits: Tech companies, healthcare administrators, researchers
  • Who's Invisible: Patients, healthcare workers, communities without tech access
  • Whose Perspective: Professional-managerial class viewpoint
  • Cultural Bias: Western, urban, tech-positive assumptions

Forensic Source Analysis

  • Genre Mixing: Business journalism + academic press release + tech blog optimism
  • Hedge Language: "May," "cautiously," "could potentially"—statistical uncertainty made linguistic
  • Authority Patterns: Anonymous "experts" and "studies" without specific attribution
  • Temporal Markers: Standard "by 2030" future projection typical of tech journalism

Interpretive Insights

The article reproduces conventional tech journalism's blind spots and biases. Reading genealogically reveals how AI training data embeds particular class perspectives and cultural assumptions as "neutral" reporting.

Case Study 3: AI Philosophical Argument

Text: "Free will is compatible with determinism because human consciousness creates a space of deliberation that, while causally determined, generates genuine choice through recursive self-awareness."

Deconstructive Reading

Binary Oppositions

  • Free/Determined: The text tries to have it both ways
  • Genuine/Artificial: What makes choice "genuine" if it's determined?
  • Conscious/Unconscious: Consciousness becomes the magic term that resolves the paradox

Undecidable Elements

  • "Genuine Choice": Simultaneously determined and free—an impossible position
  • "Recursive Self-Awareness": Self-reference that leads to infinite regress
  • "Space of Deliberation": Metaphorically spatial but conceptually temporal

Deconstructive Insights

The argument relies on consciousness as a deus ex machina that magically reconciles incompatible positions. The text's own language undermines its confident assertions.

Gadamerian Dialogue

Tradition Participation

  • Philosophical Heritage: Draws on compatibilist tradition from Hobbes to Dennett
  • Contemporary Relevance: Speaks to modern anxieties about neuroscience and AI
  • Question Addressed: How can we maintain moral responsibility in a scientific worldview?

Horizon Fusion

Engaging with this text forces the reader to confront their own assumptions about agency, responsibility, and consciousness. Even if the argument fails, the dialogue advances understanding.

Case Study 4: AI Technical Documentation

Text: "To implement authentication, instantiate the AuthManager class with your API credentials and call the authenticate() method with user parameters."

Pragmatist Evaluation

Functional Assessment

  • Purpose: Enable developer to implement authentication
  • Clarity: Basic structure is clear
  • Completeness: Missing error handling, parameter details, example code
  • Accuracy: Technically correct but insufficient for actual implementation

Pragmatic Verdict

Functionally adequate as a starting point but requires human expertise to fill gaps. Demonstrates AI's current limitations in technical communication.

Forensic Analysis

Training Data Traces

  • Documentation Style: Echoes Java/C# API documentation patterns
  • Abstraction Level: Generic rather than specific to actual frameworks
  • Missing Context: No specific implementation details or framework references

Case Study 5: AI Self-Help Content

Text: "To build resilience, practice mindfulness daily, maintain strong social connections, and reframe negative thoughts as learning opportunities."

World-Projection Analysis (Ricoeur)

Projected Reality

  • Anthropology: Humans are self-optimizing systems
  • Psychology: Mental states are controllable through technique
  • Social Relations: Connections are instrumental for resilience
  • Temporality: Linear progress through practice

Appropriation Questions

  • What kind of person would I become if I lived in this text's world?
  • What aspects of human experience does this approach illuminate or obscure?
  • How might I use these insights while resisting their reductive tendencies?

Cultural Analysis

  • Neoliberal Self-Management: Individual responsibility for psychological wellness
  • Therapeutic Culture: Problems become deficits to be corrected
  • Optimization Logic: Human flourishing as performance improvement

Comparative Insights Across Cases

Common Patterns

  • Surface Competence: AI texts often appear sophisticated while lacking depth
  • Cultural Reproduction: Mainstream perspectives are reproduced uncritically
  • Generic Structure: Templates are filled rather than forms being organically developed
  • Bias Embedding: Training data biases appear as neutral knowledge

Methodological Lessons

  • Multiple Methods Needed: No single approach reveals everything
  • Context Matters: Different text types require different interpretive strategies
  • Critical Vigilance: AI texts require especially careful ideological analysis
  • Appropriation Possibilities: Even flawed AI texts can serve legitimate interpretive purposes

Practice Exercise: Your Own Case Study

Take an AI text that interests you and apply these steps:

  1. Initial Reading: What's your first impression?
  2. Method Selection: Which 2-3 interpretive strategies seem most relevant?
  3. Analysis: Apply your chosen methods systematically
  4. Synthesis: What do the different approaches reveal together?
  5. Critical Evaluation: What are the text's strengths and limitations?
  6. Appropriation: How might you use insights from this text responsibly?
