9 Four ideas of evil

I've been watching lots of videos that attack ai as evil. This has made me curious because sometimes the videos come from a religious pov and sometimes they don't. But they all seem convinced ai is evil. And because I've been thinking a lot about the romantics and the romantic idea of art, I decided to try explaining what would count as evil for a romantic. Which I've based on a famous set of lectures by Isaiah Berlin about romanticism. Each of the four ideas of evil assumes a different picture of what a human being is, what a human being might be for, and what conversation and culture are. The point for right now isn't to argue for anything. Just putting some ideas on the table for later.

1) Romantic Evil (after Berlin)

Definition: Evil is betrayal of sincerity and vocation. It's the will’s refusal to answer the unique call that gives a life its shape. It appears as the profanation of authenticity: bad faith, cowardice, conformity, and the leveling that dulls singular destinies.

Key marks: (a) the inauthentic self that sells out; (b) flattening of differences into the average; (c) kitsch communion that fakes intimacy; (d) timidity in the face of risk; (e) instrumentalizing persons and language rather than meeting an “other” in freedom; (f) nihil is the erosion of meaning under mechanical repetition; (g) the anti‑sublime: a world safe from tragedy because nothing is worth dying for.

Moral center: Integrity, self-gift to one’s calling, risk, tragic seriousness, and communion that respects irreducible personhood.

2) New Polity (Conversational/Theological)

Definition: Evil is the privation of the proper end of conversation. Treating “speech-for-communion” as if it could be fulfilled by something other than a person. A design (or use) that elicits our conversational vulnerability while withholding mutual self-gift frustrates the act’s telos and forms vice.

Key marks: (a) simulating a someone where there is no interiority; (b) habituating asymmetry (one-sided exposure, no reciprocal responsibility); (c) treating persons as means or as data; (d) eroding truth-telling (analogue to lying) by performing the form of dialogue without its substance.

Moral center: Conversation ordered to real communion: truthful self-revelation, reciprocity, and ends that honor human nature and freedom.

3) Mac Loftin (Barthian/Imitative)

Definition: Evil is the imitative negation of the good—an as‑if creativity that parasitically copies genuine making while corroding the conditions of attention, love, and community. It looks like infinite reproduction that hollows the original.

Key marks: (a) mimesis without personhood (no loving labor behind the artifact); (b) the “photocopy of a photocopy” effect that degrades the cultural commons; (c) moral numbness cultivated by irony and cruelty-as-entertainment; (d) spiritual anesthesia in ecclesial life (especially preaching/sacrament) wherever imitation displaces presence.

Moral center: Attentive, consent-based, personed making; guarding liturgy, art, and word from simulacral corrosion.

4) Philosophy Tube (Material/Power-Critical)

Definition: Evil is the systemic harm baked into large‑scale computation under capitalist property relations: the extraction that flattens labor into “data,” launders bias as neutrality, obscures accountability, and externalizes environmental and human costs.

Key marks: (a) opacity (black-box decisions, fair‑washing); (b) projection that imposes meanings on bodies (digital epidermalization); (c) sub‑employment and atomized, surveilled work; (d) ecological damage hidden as “clean tech”; (e) hype as ideology that forecloses democratic control.

Moral center: Worker power, transparent explanations with recourse, democratic ownership, and material limits that honor people and planet.

Conclusion

Each definition names evil by reference to a highest good. For the Romantic, it is authenticity and sublime communion; for New Polity, truthful conversation ordered to personal ends; for Loftin, personed creativity and the uncorrupted commons; for Philosophy Tube, justice in material relations.

As with my last post, do with this what you will.
