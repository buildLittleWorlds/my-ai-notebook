Distanciation & AI

How does Ricoeur’s idea of distanciation help us read AI-generated texts?

Paul Ricoeur’s account of distanciation starts from a simple observation: when discourse is fixed as writing, it becomes semantically autonomous. The written text no longer depends on the author’s psychology, the original situation of speech, or a specific addressee. Instead, it projects a “world of the text” that readers appropriate in interpretation.

Why Distanciation Matters for AI

AI systems generate sequences of language that look like discourse but were not “spoken” by a subject. That matters. It sharpens what distanciation already does with writing and raises new questions about intention, reference, and readership.

What Is Distanciation?

Ricoeur argued that when speech becomes writing, the act of writing “fixes” discourse and yields a threefold autonomy: distance from the author’s intention, from the original situation of discourse, and from the original addressee.

What Writing Does: Ricoeur’s Threefold Autonomy

  1. From Intention to Meaning: What the text says can outrun what the author meant
  2. From Situation to World-of-the-Text: The text projects its own world, not the original context
  3. From Original Addressee to Anyone-Who-Can-Read: A written text addresses an unknown reader beyond the initial audience

How AI Changes (and Clarifies) the Picture

1. From Speech to Generation

Unlike human writing that typically begins from speech-like intention and is then fixed as text, AI texts were never “spoken” at all. They emerge from algorithmic processing at generation time rather than a human act of composition at a determinate moment.

  • No Human Moment of Composition: There was no “time of composition” in the human sense (though the system generates at a timestamp)
  • Infinite Reproducibility: The same prompt can generate endless variations
  • Temporal Compression: Centuries of human discourse compressed into millisecond generation
  • Always Already Written (rhetorically): AI texts exist in a state of permanent writing without ever having been “said”

2. From Non-Intention to Pure Meaning

Ricoeur argued that textual meaning transcends authorial psychology. AI starkly illustrates this point: we interpret from a position of no intention, making meaning purely textual.

  • Zero Intention: No human consciousness intended these specific words
  • Emergent Meaning: Significance arises from the text’s internal relations and its “world”
  • Reader Work: Appropriation becomes explicit labor: we “make the text our own”

3. From Reference to World-of-the-Text

AI text is detached from a lived speech situation; reference is mediated by training distributions. Distanciation pushes us to read the text’s projected world, not to chase a non-existent speaker.

  • World Projection: Focus on how the text configures a world for understanding
  • Distributed Sources: Training data stands in for “experience,” but is not itself the referent
  • Verification Loop: Claims require external checking; interpretation and fact-checking separate

4. From Intended Audience to Anyone-Who-Can-Read

Writing always expands the audience beyond the original hearer. With AI, the “reader” was always unknown: there never was an intended addressee in the human sense.

  • Open Address: The text is for anyone who can read it
  • Use-Case Driven: Prompts shape expected readers, but texts remain open
  • Publicness by Default: Generated texts circulate without any private origin

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • Separate Interpretation from Provenance: Don’t chase a non-existent author; read the text’s world
  • Make Appropriation Explicit: Own your reading; state what the text lets you see or do
  • Use Explanation and Understanding: Analyze form/distributional patterns (explanation) and pursue meaning (understanding)
  • Audit Claims: Treat factual assertions as hypotheses to verify

FAQ

Isn’t distanciation just “death of the author”?

Not quite. Distanciation is a function of writing itself, not a denial of authors entirely. Ricoeur keeps authors, but insists that meaning isn’t reducible to their psychology.

How do I cite AI texts?

Treat them as published objects: include system, model, date/time, prompt context, and version if known. Cite like software + text.
