Reception & Form Diagnostics

A practical method for reading AI texts when there is no author to reconstruct.

What this is: A toolkit grounded in Jauss (reception), Iser (implied reader & gaps), and Genette (narrative & paratexts). Use it to make disciplined, defensible interpretations of machine-written discourse.

What this is not: A detector. You won’t prove authorship; you’ll explain how a text means for readers in context.

Step 1 — Paratext Audit (Genette)

Identify the thresholds that steer reading before you even encounter the body text.

  • System & prompt: Extract the exact prompt/system message. Treat these as voice-setting paratexts.
  • UI & disclaimers: Titles, safety banners, capability notes, model cards, timestamps.
  • Provenance notes: “Generated by…,” “assisted by…,” or absence thereof.

Step 2 — Narrative Pass (Genette)

  • Order: In what sequence are events told? Any analepses/prolepses?
  • Duration & frequency: What is condensed, dilated, or repeated?
  • Mood (focalization): Who sees/knows? Zero, internal, or external focalization?
  • Voice: Who speaks? What person, distance, or embedded narrators?

Step 3 — Reader Pass (Iser)

  • Gaps (Leerstellen): Where does the text require you to fill in reference, motive, world detail?
  • Implied reader: What competencies are presupposed (genre, domain, platform lore)?
  • Concretization: Record multiple plausible “complete” readings; compare their stability.

Step 4 — Reception Pass (Jauss)

  • Horizon of expectations: What do the relevant communities expect of this genre/platform?
  • Aesthetic distance: Is the text conservative, transformative, or disruptive against that horizon?
  • Trajectory: How might reception evolve across time/releases/versions?

Step 5 — Application & Ethics

  • Augustine’s charity: Prefer interpretations that build truth/charity in community.
  • Ricoeur’s world of the text: What way of being-in-the-world does the text disclose?

Worked Micro-Example

Prompt: “Write a first-person detective monologue set on Mars.” — The prompt is a paratext that assigns voice and genre. In the output, internal focalization and compressed duration signal noir pastiche. Recurrent ellipses open gaps inviting the reader to supply backstory. On first publication, communities accustomed to golden-age hardboiled tropes may experience low aesthetic distance; a twist toward Martian geology jargon could increase distance and re-set the horizon.
